WHAT TO EAT IN SINGAPORE

Nasi Lemak

Nasi lemak is a dish that comprises rice made fragrant with coconut cream and pandan leaves. It is traditionally accompanied by fried anchovies, sliced cucumbers, otah, fried chicken, and a sweet chili sauce.

Find the best Nasi Lemak here!
Chicken Rice

Considered one of Singapore's delicacies, chicken rice is made of tender poached chicken sliced into bite-sized pieces, served with a fragrant and flavourful rice, and accompanied by dark soya sauce, garlic-chilli and ginger dips.

Find the best Chicken Rice here!
Chilli Crab

Chilli crab is among Singapore’s greatest culinary inventions, the king of all crab dishes. It is typically serve it with mud crabs that have deliciously sweet and juicy flesh with sweet yet savoury sauces, slightly spicy and supremely satisfying.

Find the best Chilli Crab here!
Laksa

Singapore laksa is a soup made with rice noodles, seafood (commonly shrimp and cockles, or clams), a creamy curry and coconut milk based broth with plenty of seasonings and spices. The spices often include lemongrass, palm sugar, spicy chilies, tamarind and lime.

Find the best Laksa here!
Hokkien Prawn Mee

Hokkien Mee is a stir-fried noodle dish is steeped in aromatic stock made of pork bones and prawn heads. It compromises of yellow noodles and thick 'bee hoon' (vermicelli)—has juicy prawns, squid, pork belly strips, egg and crunchy fried pork lard (optional) that makes everything taste extra good. It is served with sambal chilli and a squeeze of lime juice.

Find the best Hokkien Mee here!
Roti Prata

As the name suggests, roti which literally translates to “bread” and prata means “flat”, it is a flatbread sold mostly by Indian Muslims stallholders at coffee shops and hawker centres. The two common types of pratas served are plain prata and egg prata. There are many other prata variations like cheese prata, milo prata, and ice cream prata.

Find the best Roti Prata here!
Char Kway Teow

Char Kway Teow also known as Fried Kway Teow is one of the most wanted dishes in Singapore that you cannot pass through. You may be melted with the mixture of flat rice noodles and egg noodles which will be commonly stir-fried with eggs, cockles, lap cheong (Chinese sausages), bean sprouts, and Chinese chives.

Find the best Char Kway Teow here!
Oyster Egg

Oyster omelette, or also known as Orh luak is one of Singapore's must have dishes if you go to a local Hawker Center. Starch is mixed into the egg batter while whipping up the omelette, hence giving it a thick consistency, creating a gastronomic blend of crisp that goes really well with the juicy and wild taste of oysters.

Find the best Oyster Egg here!
Bak Kut Teh

Bak kut teh, or pork ribs soup, is a popular Chinese dish in Singapore. The dish consists of pork ribs stewed with a mixture of fragrant herbs and spices such as garlic, cloves, cinnamon, star anise, fennel seeds and coriander.

Find the best Bak Kut Teh here!
Fish Head Curry

Fish head curry is unique to Singapore, a mixture of the South Indian curry spices with the fish head, a delicacy among the Chinese. There are various version of the curry. Some mix in tamarind paste for a tinge of sourness, others add coconut milk for a creamier texture. But one thing in common is that it has to be spicy, and commonly contain lady fingers and egg plants.

Find the best Fish Head Curry here!
